The law of diminishing marginal

Is it enough?

What can help us make choices in our day to day lives? Well utility should probably be considered. Everything we do, buy, or recieve gives us some form of satisfaction. Whether that's positive or negative is subjective to each person. With every unit of goods recieved, satisfaction increases. But of course, there are limits to this.

I want to introduce the law of diminishing marginal utility. It states that as consumption increases, the satisfaction you get from each additional unit decreases.

We live in a world of consumption. Trends come and go faster than ever, and we are force fed products that promise prestige among your peers, convenience, and more. As soon as you have the shiny new thing, the next is already making big waves. It can become overwhelming for a lot of people.
